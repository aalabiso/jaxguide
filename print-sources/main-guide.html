⚡ Fun Fact

Neighborhoods & History

Jacksonville isn't one neighborhood — it's dozens of distinct communities, each with its own personality, architecture, and soul. From the historic charm of Riverside to the tree-lined streets of Arlington, there's a Jax for everyone.

"Spiritualized Life" at Memorial Park

Charles Adrian Pillars' 1924 bronze masterpiece — a winged figure atop a globe — photographed during Hurricane Irma's storm surge in 2017. Memorial Park honors Jacksonville's fallen from World War I.

🔥 The Great Fire of 1901

On May 3, 1901, the Great Fire of Jacksonville destroyed 146 city blocks and 2,368 buildings in just 8 hours — leaving nearly 10,000 homeless. The city rebuilt stronger and more beautiful than before, shaping the downtown we know today.

📖 Did You Know? The area that became downtown Jacksonville was known to the Seminole people as "Wacca Pilatka" and to the British as the "Cow Ford." In 1822, local settlers platted the town and named it after Andrew Jackson — who never actually lived there.

Beaches & Outdoor Adventures

Jacksonville is blessed with more coastline than any other Florida city, plus a sprawling network of rivers, marshes, nature preserves, and parks. This is an outdoor lover's paradise — and it's at your doorstep.

The Southbank Riverwalk

1.25 miles of scenic promenade along the St. Johns River, home to the Friendship Fountain and stunning skyline views at sunset.

🌊
Jacksonville Beach
22 miles of uncrowded Atlantic shoreline with hard-packed sand. The rebuilt pier stretches a quarter mile into the ocean. Tide pools daily — great for spotting crabs.
🐋

Drive your car right onto the beach! Great Florida Birding Trail, dolphin watching, kiteboarding. Right whales sometimes spotted offshore in winter.
🌿

46,000 acres of unspoiled salt marshes and ancient forests — the largest unaltered estuary in the U.S. Southeast. Contains Fort Caroline (1564).

Legit surf culture. Atlantic swells best late summer through fall. Thompson Surf School offers lessons for all levels.

🏆 Sports Trivia

EverBank Stadium is one of only two NFL stadiums with in-ground swimming pools in the end zones. Fans can buy cabana seats and watch with cocktails. The most uniquely Jacksonville thing in existence.

⛳ Island Green — Hole 17 — Perhaps the most famous par-3 in the world. Just 137 yards, completely surrounded by water. Over 100,000 golf balls retrieved annually. The island concept was Alice Dye's suggestion — Pete wasn't sure about it. The rest is history.

🏌️ Golf History

The PGA Tour moved its headquarters from New York City to Ponte Vedra Beach in 1979, forever transforming the First Coast into America's golf epicenter. The move brought world-class architects, resort courses, and a culture of golf that permeates every corner of Jacksonville. Today, combined yardage of all 50+ area courses exceeds 350,000 yards — that's nearly 200 miles of fairway.

And Chamblin Bookmine is one of the largest independent bookstores in the Southeast — 70,000+ sq ft of books.

🎬 Film & TV: Jacksonville was once called the "Winter Film Capital of the World" — over 30 film studios operated here in the early 1900s, before Hollywood took over. Today the city is making a comeback as a filming location, and the annual Jacksonville Film Festival showcases independent cinema each fall.

Road Trips from Jax

Day Trips & Surrounding Areas

Jacksonville sits in a prime spot for day tripping. The Nation's Oldest City, a stunning island paradise, the Okefenokee Swamp, and the Disney region are all within a few hours' drive.

📍 45 Minutes South
St. Augustine 🏰
Oldest continuously occupied European city in North America (1565). Castillo de San Marcos, cobblestone streets, O'Steen's for lunch, the Lighthouse. A perfect full-day adventure with world-class history.
📍 40 Minutes North
Amelia Island 🌺
13 miles of stunning beaches, Victorian Fernandina Beach, Fort Clinch State Park, world-class resort spas. Horseback riding on the beach is an option you absolutely should not pass up.
📍 1.5 Hours West
Gainesville & Silver Springs 🌊
Crystal-clear springs with 80+ foot visibility. Glass-bottom boat tours since the 1870s. Manatees, otters, alligators. Don't miss the Cade Museum for hands-on STEAM learning.
📍 1.5 Hours North
Cumberland Island & Savannah 🐴
Ferry to Georgia's largest Sea Island — wild horses, Carnegie mansion ruins, pure untouched nature. Continue to Savannah — cobblestone squares, antebellum mansions, America's most beautiful city.
📍 1.5 Hours West
Okefenokee Swamp 🐊
438,000 acres of primordial wilderness. Guided boat tours through glass-still water reflecting Spanish moss cypress. Alligators, otters, cranes. Utterly unlike anything else.
📍 2 Hours South
Daytona Beach & Space Coast 🚀
NASCAR's Daytona 500, cars on the beach, then Cape Canaveral and Kennedy Space Center. Watch a real rocket launch if timing aligns. Awe-inspiring.
📍 2.5 Hours South
Orlando & Theme Parks 🎢
Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld — all within a comfortable day drive. Leave early, hit the parks, and be back for a Jax Beach sunset dinner.
📍 1 Hour South
Palatka & the Springs 🌿
Ravine Gardens State Park has stunning azalea trails (peak bloom February). Continue west to Devil's Den and Ichetucknee Springs for crystal-clear swimming and tubing.
📜 Day Trip History Fact

St. Augustine was founded in 1565 — 42 years before Jamestown, 55 years before Plymouth. The Castillo de San Marcos (completed 1695) has never been taken by force. Its coquina stone walls absorbed cannon fire rather than crumbling.

🐴

Don't Miss: Horseback riding on the beach at Amelia Island — one of the few places in Florida where this is still allowed. Book through Kelly Seahorse Ranch for a sunset ride along the shoreline. Unforgettable experience for all ages.

🗺️ Road Trip Pro Tip: I-95 South gets you to St. Augustine in 45 min, Daytona in 2 hours, and Orlando in 2.5. Heading north, I-95 reaches Savannah in under 2.5 hours. The A1A Scenic Highway along the coast is slower but spectacularly beautiful — consider it for the Amelia Island drive. Pack a cooler and make it an adventure.

🌴 Local Lingo: "DUUUVAL!" is the battle cry — you'll hear it at Jags games, bars, and randomly on the street. "The Beaches" means Jax Beach, Neptune Beach, and Atlantic Beach collectively. "The Southbank" and "The Northbank" refer to opposite sides of downtown along the river. "First Coast" is what locals call the greater Jacksonville region.

🚗

Getting Around: Jax is a car city — you'll want one for exploring. Downtown to the beaches is about 25 min. The St. Johns River Ferry (Mayport to Fort George Island) is a fun $7 shortcut. JTA Skyway is free downtown. Beach trolleys run seasonally along the coast.
