Jacksonville was founded in 1822 and named after Andrew Jackson — who never actually lived there. The city consolidated with Duval County in 1968, making it the largest city by area in the contiguous U.S. — bigger than NYC, LA, and Chicago combined.

⚡ Fun Fact

Jacksonville has more miles of navigable waterway than any other city in the nation — over 1,100 miles of rivers, creeks, the Intracoastal Waterway, and coastline.

🎸

Music City Roots: Jacksonville's music heritage is legendary. Lynyrd Skynyrd formed at Robert E. Lee High School in the '60s. Tom Petty played formative gigs in the area. The Allman Brothers Band practically lived at the old Comic Book Club. Today, Jax hosts the largest free jazz festival in the Southeast every Memorial Day weekend.

Neighborhoods & History

Jacksonville isn't one neighborhood — it's dozens of distinct communities, each with its own personality, architecture, and soul. From the historic charm of Riverside to the tree-lined streets of Arlington, there's a Jax for everyone.

"Spiritualized Life" at Memorial Park

Charles Adrian Pillars' 1924 bronze masterpiece — a winged figure atop a globe. Memorial Park honors Jacksonville's fallen from World War I.

🔥 The Great Fire of 1901

On May 3, 1901, the Great Fire of Jacksonville destroyed 146 city blocks and 2,368 buildings in just 8 hours. The city rebuilt stronger and more beautiful than before, shaping downtown we know today.

📖 Did You Know? The area that became downtown Jacksonville was known to the Seminole people as "Wacca Pilatka" and to the British as the "Cow Ford." In 1822, local settlers platted the town and named it after Andrew Jackson.

Sun, Surf & Nature

Beaches & Outdoor Adventures

Jacksonville is blessed with more coastline than any other Florida city, plus a sprawling network of rivers, marshes, nature preserves, and parks. This is an outdoor lover's paradise.

The Southbank Riverwalk

1.25 miles of scenic promenade along the St. Johns River, home to the Friendship Fountain and stunning skyline views at sunset.

🌊
Jacksonville Beach
22 miles of uncrowded Atlantic shoreline with hard-packed sand. The rebuilt pier stretches a quarter mile. Tide pools daily — great for spotting crabs.
🐋
Huguenot Memorial Park
Drive your car right onto the beach! Great Florida Birding Trail, dolphin watching, kiteboarding. Right whales sometimes spotted offshore in winter.
🌿
Timucuan Preserve
46,000 acres of unspoiled salt marshes and ancient forests — the largest unaltered estuary in the U.S. Southeast. Contains Fort Caroline (1564).
🏄
Surfing at Jax Beach
Legit surf culture. Atlantic swells best late summer through fall. Thompson Surf School offers lessons for all levels.
🦅
Talbot Islands
Some of the most beautiful unspoiled beaches on the East Coast. Little Talbot is one of Florida's last undeveloped barrier islands.
🚣
Kayaking & Paddleboarding
1,100 miles of navigable water. Paddle to dolphin feeding grounds at the mouth of the St. Johns. Rentals available throughout the beaches.
🎣
Fishing Charters
Deep sea, inshore, and river fishing year-round. Mayport is the launching point. Red drum, flounder, sheepshead, and kingfish in season.
🐢
Guana River State Park
2,400 acres of pristine beach, dunes, and salt marsh. Uncrowded swimming, hiking trails, and a nature center. Sea turtles nest May–October.
🚲
Beach Bike Rentals
Cruise the oceanfront on a beach cruiser or electric bike. Multiple rental shops along 1st Street. Flat terrain, ocean breezes, pure bliss.
🦈

Shark Teeth Alert! Mickler's Landing in Ponte Vedra Beach is one of the best spots in the Southeast to find fossilized shark teeth. Bring a sifter — kids and adults go wild!

🏆 Sports Trivia

EverBank Stadium is one of only two NFL stadiums with in-ground swimming pools in the end zones. Fans can buy cabana seats and watch with cocktails. The most uniquely Jacksonville thing in existence.

🏈 Game Day Culture: The "DUUUVAL!" chant echoes through EverBank on Sundays — one side yells "DUU" and the other answers "VAL!" Tailgating opens 5 hours before kickoff. The Florida-Georgia game has filled the stadium every October since 1933.

⛳ Island Green — Hole 17 — Perhaps the most famous par-3 in the world. Just 137 yards, completely surrounded by water. Over 100,000 golf balls retrieved annually. The island concept was Alice Dye's suggestion — Pete wasn't sure. The rest is history.

🏌️ Golf History

The PGA Tour moved its headquarters from New York City to Ponte Vedra Beach in 1979, forever transforming the First Coast into America's golf epicenter. Combined yardage of all 50+ courses exceeds 350,000 yards — that's nearly 200 miles of fairway.

🎸 Music Trivia

Lynyrd Skynyrd formed here in the 1960s (named after their PE teacher Leonard Skinner). The Allman Brothers honed their skills in Jax. Tom Petty played formative shows here. Jacksonville's music DNA runs deep.

🎨

🎬 Film & TV: Jacksonville was once called the "Winter Film Capital of the World" — over 30 film studios operated here in the early 1900s, before Hollywood took over. The annual Jacksonville Film Festival showcases independent cinema each fall.

Road Trips from Jax

Day Trips & Surrounding Areas

Jacksonville sits in a prime spot for day tripping. The Nation's Oldest City, a stunning island paradise, the Okefenokee Swamp, and the Disney region are all within a few hours' drive.

📍 45 Minutes South
St. Augustine 🏰
Oldest continuously occupied European city in North America (1565). Castillo de San Marcos, cobblestone streets, O'Steen's for lunch. A perfect full-day adventure.
📍 40 Minutes North
Amelia Island 🌺
13 miles of stunning beaches, Victorian Fernandina Beach, Fort Clinch State Park, world-class resort spas. Horseback riding on the beach is an option!
📍 1.5 Hours West
Gainesville & Silver Springs 🌊
Crystal-clear springs with 80+ foot visibility. Glass-bottom boat tours since the 1870s. Manatees, otters, alligators. Don't miss the Cade Museum.
📍 1.5 Hours North
Cumberland Island & Savannah 🐴
Ferry to Georgia's largest Sea Island — wild horses, Carnegie mansion ruins, pure nature. Continue to Savannah — cobblestone squares, antebellum mansions.
📍 1.5 Hours West
Okefenokee Swamp 🐊
438,000 acres of primordial wilderness. Guided boat tours through glass-still water reflecting Spanish moss cypress. Alligators, otters, cranes.
📍 2 Hours South
Daytona Beach & Space Coast 🚀
NASCAR's Daytona 500, cars on the beach, then Cape Canaveral and Kennedy Space Center. Watch a real rocket launch if timing aligns.
📍 2.5 Hours South
Orlando & Theme Parks 🎢
Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld — all within a comfortable day drive. Leave early, hit the parks, be back for sunset dinner.
📍 1 Hour South
Palatka & the Springs 🌿
Ravine Gardens State Park has stunning azalea trails (peak bloom February). Devil's Den and Ichetucknee Springs for crystal-clear swimming and tubing.
📜 Day Trip History Fact

St. Augustine was founded in 1565 — 42 years before Jamestown, 55 years before Plymouth. The Castillo de San Marcos (completed 1695) has never been taken by force. Its coquina stone walls absorbed cannon fire rather than crumbling.

🐴

Don't Miss: Horseback riding on the beach at Amelia Island — one of the few places in Florida where this is allowed. Book through Kelly Seahorse Ranch for a sunset ride along the shoreline. Unforgettable for all ages.

🗺️ Road Trip Pro Tip: I-95 South gets you to St. Augustine in 45 min, Daytona in 2 hours, and Orlando in 2.5. Heading north, I-95 reaches Savannah in under 2.5 hours. The A1A Scenic Highway along the coast is slower but spectacularly beautiful — consider it for the Amelia Island drive.
